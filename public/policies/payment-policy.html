Rep Payment Workflow

How and when sales reps are paid for submissions raised via the rep portal — the submission window, the payout cadence, and the case-by-case exception for early payment.

Effective [date] Owner: Finance / Sales Ops Reviewed annually
01

Purpose

This policy explains how reps are paid for submissions raised via the rep portal: the monthly submission window, the relationship between client payment and rep payout, and the rare circumstances under which an early payment can be authorised.

It exists to give reps clear, predictable visibility on when money will land, while protecting the business from paying on revenue that hasn't yet cleared.

02

The payment workflow

Three calendar months in play at any one time. The work that earns the payout happens in the previous month; the submission is raised in the current month; the payout lands in the following month.

Previous month
Clients pay invoices

Only invoices the client has actually paid during the previous calendar month qualify. Issued-but-unpaid invoices do not count.

→
Current month · days 1–14
Rep submits via the rep portal

Within the first 14 days of the current month, the rep submits the previous-month paid invoices through the rep portal. Submissions raised after day 14 roll into the next monthly cycle.

→
Following month
Payout lands in rep's account

Finance processes the verified submissions and pays the rep during the following calendar month. Default cadence — no per-deal expediting.

04

Worked example Illustrative

Scenario: a client pays an invoice on 23 January.

The rep submits this paid invoice via the rep portal at some point during 1–14 February. Finance processes the submission, verifies the underlying payment cleared, and the rep is paid out during March.

If the rep raises the submission on 17 February (after the day-14 cut-off), it falls outside the February window. It rolls into the next cycle — submitted again during 1–14 March, paid out during April.

05

Definitions & rules

"Paid invoice"

An invoice for which the client's payment has been received and cleared into The Good Foundry's bank account. An invoice that is issued but unpaid, partially paid, or in dispute is not a paid invoice for this purpose.

The 14-day submission window

The rep portal accepts submissions from day 1 through day 14 of the current calendar month for invoices paid in the previous month. Submissions raised on or after day 15 are queued into the following month's window. The portal timestamp is the official record.

The +1-month payout

Finance verifies submissions during the back half of the current month and processes payouts during the following calendar month. Reps see the payment in their nominated account on or before the last business day of that month.

Multiple invoices in one submission

Reps may bundle every paid invoice from the previous month into a single submission. Bundling does not change the payout cadence — verification and payout still happen in the same monthly cycle.

06

Exception — early payment

Reserved for genuinely exceptional circumstances. Not a deferred benefit reps can lean on.

By arrangement with management

Early payment can be requested on a case-by-case basis

Upon arrangement with management, an exception to the standard +1-month payout cadence can be granted on a case-by-case basis only. There is no automatic entitlement. Each request is considered on its own merits and approval is at management's discretion.

To request an exception, the rep raises the conversation with the responsible sales leader or finance contact before the submission would otherwise sit waiting for the standard cycle. Management evaluates the specific situation — the underlying business reason, the size and certainty of the cleared payment, the impact of expediting on cash-flow planning — and either approves or declines the request.

Approved exceptions are documented against the submission record so the audit trail stays clean.

07

Governance

Submission timestamps, payment verification, and payout records are maintained by Finance. Disputes are resolved against the rep portal record and the underlying client payment record. Because both are timestamped, the system of record is the single source of truth.

This policy is reviewed annually. Material changes to the cadence (submission window, payout delay) are communicated to all reps at least one full cycle before they take effect.
