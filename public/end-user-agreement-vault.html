5Your Information and Privacy

How we collect, use, store, and disclose Personal Information is set out in full in the Privacy Policy, which forms part of this Agreement. Please read it. In summary, and in respect of your Responses:

  1. Roles. The Customer decides why the survey is run and is the controller of your Responses; The Good Foundry acts as the Customer’s processor for those Responses. The Good Foundry is the controller of the limited Personal Information it needs to operate the Platform itself — namely to authenticate you, send you invitations and reminders, and provide your Vault.
  2. Your Vault. You can see your own individual results in your Vault. Administrators see results in de-identified, aggregated form only, subject to minimum-group-size thresholds; they do not see your individual responses attributed to you.
  3. De-identified benchmarking. We may retain and use de-identified, aggregated data (which cannot reasonably be used to identify you) to generate population scores, benchmarks, and to improve the Platform, as disclosed in the Privacy Policy.
  4. Hosting. Your data is stored in the hosting region closest to your location — Sydney, Singapore, Iowa, London, Frankfurt, or Eemshaven — with Personal Information and Response data held separately. Where data leaves its region (for example, for support), recognised cross-border transfer mechanisms apply, as described in the Privacy Policy and the Customer’s Data Processing Agreement.
  5. Your rights. Subject to applicable law, you may request access to, correction of, deletion of, or a copy of your Personal Information, or object to or restrict its use, by contacting privacy@the-good-foundry.com. We facilitate such requests with the Customer where the Customer is the controller.
  6. Sensitive information. Some questions invite sensitive information (for example, about how you are feeling, or demographic details). Where you choose to provide it, you consent to our processing it to provide the Platform; you may decline any such question.

It does not provide medical, psychological, or clinical advice, does not diagnose or treat any condition, and is not a substitute for care from a qualified professional.
