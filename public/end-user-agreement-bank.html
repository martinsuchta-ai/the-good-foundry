End User Agreement

This End User Agreement (the “Agreement”) governs your use of the administrator console (the “Bank”) of {{PLATFORM_NAME}} (the “Platform”), operated by {{OWNER_LEGAL}} (“The Good Foundry”, “we”, “us” or “our”). It applies to you as an individual Administrator (“you” or “your”) acting on behalf of the organisation that has procured the Platform (the “Customer”).

This Agreement sets the rules for the individual administrator account. It operates alongside, and is subject to, the Master Services Agreement (MSA) and Data Processing Agreement (DPA) between The Good Foundry and the Customer, which govern the commercial relationship and the processing of personal data. Where this Agreement conflicts with the MSA or DPA, the MSA or DPA (as applicable) prevails.

1Definitions

In this Agreement:

Administrator An individual issued with credentials to access and operate the Customer’s Bank account — that is, you.
Aggregate Results Results presented in de-identified, grouped form, subject to minimum-group-size thresholds, such that an individual Respondent cannot reasonably be identified.
Customer The organisation that has procured the Platform under the MSA and on whose behalf you act.
DPA / MSA The Data Processing Agreement and Master Services Agreement between The Good Foundry and the Customer.
Privacy Policy The Good Foundry’s Privacy Policy at https://www.the-good-foundry.com/privacy-policy.html, as updated from time to time.
Respondent An individual invited to respond using the Platform.

2Acceptance and Authority

  1. By accessing or using the Bank — including by signing in or performing any administrative action — you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Agreement and the Privacy Policy.
  2. You confirm that you are authorised by the Customer to act as an Administrator and to perform the functions in clause 4 on its behalf, and that your use complies with the Customer’s own policies.
  3. A current copy of this Agreement is linked from the footer of the Bank so that it is available to you before and at the time of use. If you do not agree to it, do not access the Bank.

3Your Administrator Account and Authentication

  1. Your administrator account is personal to you. You must keep your credentials secure, must not share them, and must not allow anyone else to use your account. Where single sign-on (SSO) is enabled, you authenticate against the Customer’s identity provider and we do not receive your password.
  2. You are responsible for activity carried out under your account. Notify us promptly at privacy@the-good-foundry.com if you believe your account has been compromised.
  3. Administrative actions are logged. Access to Customer production data by The Good Foundry personnel is limited to client-initiated support, implementation, and critical incident response, is logged, and is retained as described in the MSA and Technical FAQ.

4Administrator Functions and Permitted Use

Within the Customer’s subscription and entitlements, the Bank lets you:

  • invite Respondents and manage collection rounds, reminders, and survey configuration;
  • create, manage, and remove (including delete) other Administrator users within the Customer’s account;
  • view, filter, and export Aggregate Results, including via CSV / JSON export or any data-warehouse feed enabled for the Customer;
  • configure editions, add-ons, themes, and account settings available under the subscription.
  1. You may use the Bank and any results only for the Customer’s legitimate wellbeing purposes, and in accordance with this Agreement, the MSA, the DPA, the Privacy Policy, and applicable law.
  2. Individual responses are not visible. The Bank surfaces results to Administrators in Aggregate form only, subject to minimum-group-size thresholds. You must not attempt to access, derive, or reconstruct any individual Respondent’s identified responses.
  3. You must not use the Platform to make decisions about an identified individual’s employment, performance, discipline, study, or care on the basis of their participation or their individual wellbeing responses.

5Confidentiality and Respondent Privacy

  1. You must treat Aggregate Results and any export as confidential information of the Customer and the Respondents, store and transmit them securely, and limit access to people within the Customer who have a genuine need to know.
  2. You must not attempt to re-identify any Respondent, nor combine Aggregate Results with other data in a way that could single out an individual, nor circumvent minimum-group-size thresholds.
  3. You must respect the anonymity and voluntary nature of participation, and must not pressure any individual to respond or to disclose their individual results.

6Data-Protection Responsibilities

  1. As between The Good Foundry and the Customer, the Customer is the controller of Respondent response data and The Good Foundry is its processor, as set out in the DPA. The full detail of how personal information is handled is in the Privacy Policy and the DPA.
  2. Before inviting Respondents, you are responsible (on behalf of the Customer) for ensuring there is a lawful basis to do so and that any notices or consents required by applicable law have been given or obtained — including, where an edition is used with minors, the consents addressed in Schedule 1.
  3. You will pass through and assist with Respondent data-subject requests (access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, objection) in accordance with the DPA, and will direct any request you receive to privacy@the-good-foundry.com where The Good Foundry’s action is required.

7Intellectual Property

  1. The Platform, its software, the PERMA+ framework as implemented, question banks, dashboards, and branding are owned by The Good Foundry or its licensors. You are granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable right to use the Bank for the Customer’s internal purposes for the duration of the subscription.
  2. The Customer retains ownership of its data. The Good Foundry may retain and use de-identified, aggregated benchmark data for cross-client benchmarking and to improve the Platform, as disclosed in the Privacy Policy and the MSA.
  3. You must not copy, resell, sub-license, or make the Platform available to any third party, except as the MSA expressly permits.

8Limitation of Liability

  1. The commercial allocation of liability between The Good Foundry and the Customer is governed by the MSA. As between The Good Foundry and you as an individual Administrator, and to the maximum extent permitted by law, The Good Foundry is not liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential loss, or any loss of profits, data, or goodwill.
  2. Nothing in this Agreement excludes, restricts, or modifies any guarantee, right, or remedy that cannot lawfully be excluded or limited, including under the Australian Consumer Law and any equivalent UK, EU, or US law.
  3. The Platform is provided on an “as is” and “as available” basis, subject to the service commitments in the MSA. It supports wellbeing measurement and is not a medical, diagnostic, or crisis service.

9Indemnity

To the maximum extent permitted by law, the Customer (and you, to the extent of your own acts or omissions) indemnifies The Good Foundry against loss arising from:

  • your breach of this Agreement, including clauses 4, 5, and 6;
  • inviting Respondents without the lawful basis, notices, or consents required;
  • any attempt to re-identify Respondents or misuse of results; and
  • any unlawful instruction you give in operating the Bank.

This clause is to be read with, and is subject to, the indemnity and liability provisions of the MSA, which prevail in case of conflict.

10Term and Termination

  1. This Agreement applies from when you first access the Bank and continues while you are an authorised Administrator and the Customer’s subscription is current.
  2. Your access ends when the Customer withdraws your authorisation, when another Administrator removes your account, or when the subscription ends or is terminated under the MSA. We may suspend access where we reasonably believe this Agreement has been breached.
  3. On termination, the Customer may request a full export of its data in JSON or CSV at no cost, and The Good Foundry will irreversibly delete the data and provide a deletion certificate within the period stated in the DPA. Clauses 5, 6 (accrued), 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12 survive termination.
