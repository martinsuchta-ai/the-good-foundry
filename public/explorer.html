Quietly Cracking NEW

A 22-item ME / WE / US assessment plus three free-text reflections that surfaces high-functioning burnout — people still meeting deadlines but running on empty. Three protective-factor items (someone to talk to, organisational mental-health support, openness to discuss wellbeing) are reverse-scored so a HIGH score across the dashboard always reads as more quietly cracking. Available across Workplace, Life and University editions.

Who it’s for

HR teams running annual or quarterly wellbeing programs. Safety leads aligning to the new WHS psychosocial-hazard regulations. People-and-culture executives wanting cohort dashboards across LoB / contract / role. Used by EAPs offering wellbeing assessment as part of a member benefit.

Wellbeing Matters In Aging Well

WAW · Wellbeing Matters In Aging Well

Wellbeing Matters In Aging Well

PERMAH+ reframed for adults navigating later life.
Aging Well
What it measures
  • Wellbeing — PERMAH+ across calm, engagement, meaning, accomplishment, health
  • Nine Amplifiers — Ability · Motivation · Psych Safety · Emotional Resilience · Knowing Me · Inclusion & Belonging · Maintaining Purpose · Learning & Growing · Optimizing Health
  • Self-Compassion — Self-Talk · Mindfulness · Connection
  • Life Satisfaction + State of Wellbeing selector
  • Modifiable Risk Reflection (optional) — the 14 Lancet-2024 dementia risk factors on a past · current · future matrix
Modifiable Risk Reflection NEW

A single optional matrix question covering all 14 modifiable dementia risk factors from the 2024 Lancet Commission — hearing, smoking, hypertension, alcohol, obesity, depression, social isolation, physical inactivity, air pollution, head injury, less education, diabetes, untreated vision loss, untreated cholesterol — rated across past, current, and future intent. Cohort-level dashboard surfaces risk distribution, top co-occurrence patterns, and the per-factor breakdown so program leads can target interventions. Privacy gates: n≥30 cohort minimum, n≥5 cell minimum.

Who it’s for

LiveWell residential programs. Aged-care providers running wellbeing assessments. Third-age university programs. Family and friend supporter networks. Community programs for adults 55+. The Modifiable Risk Reflection section makes this edition uniquely valuable for programs aligned to dementia-risk-reduction goals.
