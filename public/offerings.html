Featured Bespoke
WHC · The HEART Of Change Tool

The HEART Of Change ToolNEW · 2026

Built with The Change Lab · Dr. Michelle McQuaid’s HEART Framework

A bespoke edition for organisations navigating change. Built on Dr. Michelle McQuaid’s HEART Framework — five evidence-based factors that help us regulate our nervous systems and create psychological safety during times of uncertainty: Honour Feelings · Engage Purposefully · Appreciate Strengths · Reach Out · Take Tiny Steps. The 7-page survey measures each factor across three tiers (Individual / Team / Workplace), surfaces the four-quadrant State of Change (Capable / Challenged / Unsettled / Vulnerable) on a Safety × Predictability matrix, and reports Change AMPlifiers (Ability / Motivation / Psych Safety / Self-Compassion) and Workplace Outcomes (Job Sat / Performance / Safety / Wellbeing) alongside a configurable general-population baseline. Individual respondents see their own HEART profile in their vault; admins see the cohort dashboard with State-of-Change distribution, factor-by-quadrant breakdowns, and a verbatim feedback feed with auto-redacted PII.

Quietly Cracking NEW

A 22-item ME/WE/US check-in that surfaces the "high-functioning burnout" pattern — respondents who still meet deadlines and show up day to day, but are running on empty, masking it, and quietly disengaging. Three free-text reflection prompts give people the space to say what the sliders can't. Available across Workplace, Life and University editions.

Annual

Digital Wellbeing NEW

A 15-item ME/WE/US check-in for school-age respondents on how phones, social media, gaming and screen time are shaping wellbeing. ME captures personal habits (sleep impact, scrolling fatigue, ability to put devices down), WE captures peer signals (comparison, cyberbullying, online friendships), and US captures whether the school and adults around them help navigate it well. Seven protective-factor items are reverse-scored so a HIGH score always reads as MORE digital strain. Includes a PERMA+ Impact matrix in the bank dashboard — splits the cohort into Low / Mid / High strain tertiles and shows mean PERMA+ per tertile, so leadership can see which wellbeing pillars correlate most strongly with digital strain. Available across the Kids and Youth school editions, with separate question banks tuned to each age group's language.

Annual

Social Drivers NEW

A SCARF-informed measure of the five social drivers that decide whether people feel they belong somewhere — Valued (do I matter here?), Clarity (do I know what’s coming?), Choice (do I have a say?), Connection (am I among allies?) and a Fair go (is the exchange fair?). Each driver is rated across three levels — Me (the individual), We (the team) and Us (the organisation) — so you can see exactly where belonging, psychological safety and trust break down between how someone feels personally, inside their team, and about the organisation as a whole. Results read as clear threat / mixed / reward zones with me→we→us trust gradients, an individual belonging report in the vault, and an organisation-wide heatmap in the bank. Available across every edition — workplaces, universities, sport, veterinary practice, aged care and personal life — because the need to feel valued, informed, trusted with a say, connected to others and fairly treated is human, not just a workplace concern.
