Dr. Martin E.P. Seligman

University of Pennsylvania

The PERMA Model of Flourishing

Our Wellbeing Matters program is informed by the PERMA model of flourishing — a theory of wellbeing developed by Dr. Seligman. PERMA proposes that human flourishing is supported by five measurable elements: Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment.

We gratefully acknowledge Dr. Seligman as the originator of this wellbeing theory, which continues to inform the methodology underpinning our work.

Reference Seligman, M.E.P. (2011). Flourish: A Visionary New Understanding of Happiness and Well-being. New York: Free Press.
Conceived the PERMAH Survey

Dr. Michelle McQuaid

Wellbeing researcher and practitioner
Translator of PERMAH into applied measurement

From PERMA to PERMAH

Dr. McQuaid conceived the PERMAH Wellbeing Survey and led its development as an applied wellbeing measurement tool, including the integration of Health as a sixth element extending Dr. Seligman’s original PERMA framework.

Her work made it possible for organisations, schools and individuals to measure flourishing in a structured, repeatable way.

Foundational Scholarly Contributor

Professor Peggy Kern

The University of Melbourne
Positive psychology · psychometrics · life-course wellbeing

Acknowledgement of Professor Peggy Kern

Professor Peggy Kern, of The University of Melbourne, is a foundational contributor to the PERMAH Wellbeing Survey and one of the leading scholarly voices in the international science of wellbeing measurement.

Professor Kern’s research spans positive psychology, life-course wellbeing, applied psychometrics, and the systems-level study of how individuals and communities flourish. Her published work has helped shape the modern measurement of wellbeing and the scientific frameworks used by researchers and practitioners worldwide.

Foundational contribution to PERMAH

In the survey’s earliest stages, Professor Kern worked alongside Dr. Michelle McQuaid and Martin Suchta as a foundational contributor to the development of the PERMAH question sets and scoring methodology. She brought scholarly rigour to the translation of the underlying theory into measurable survey items, the design of the scoring approach, and the psychometric properties that determine how the instrument behaves in practice. Her contribution established the scientific foundations on which the survey continues to rest today.

Ongoing scholarly contribution

Beyond the survey’s foundational phase, Professor Kern’s broader body of research continues to inform the scientific evolution of the framework — including the development of PERMA+ — through her contributions to the wider field of wellbeing measurement and applied positive psychology.

We gratefully acknowledge Professor Kern’s foundational and ongoing contribution to the science, design, and continued development of the framework.

Ongoing Research Leadership

Martin Suchta

Director, The Good Foundry Pty Ltd
Original contributor · PERMA+ research lead

Acknowledgement of Ongoing Research Leadership

The PERMAH Wellbeing Survey is a living research instrument, and its continued scientific development is led by Martin Suchta, Director of The Good Foundry Pty Ltd.

Original contribution

In the survey’s foundational phase, Martin worked alongside Dr. Michelle McQuaid and Dr. Peggy Kern as an original contributor to the development of the PERMAH question sets and scoring methodology. His early work helped shape the structure of the survey items, the scoring approach, and the design choices that have since enabled the instrument to be used at scale.

Ongoing research and the PERMA+ framework

Beyond the survey’s initial scope, Martin has led sustained investment in research to extend the methodology and deepen its evidence base. This work has culminated in the development of PERMA+, a methodological extension of the original PERMA framework that integrates additional dimensions of wellbeing emerging from contemporary research, longitudinal survey data, and applied practice across workplaces, schools, and communities.

PERMA+ is offered as a research framework and methodological evolution, building on — and acknowledging — Dr. Martin E.P. Seligman’s foundational PERMA theory of flourishing.

We acknowledge Martin’s foundational and ongoing contribution to the science, design, and continued development of the PERMAH Wellbeing Survey.

PERMA+ · Psychosocial Hazards & Culture of Care

Dr. Michelle McQuaid

Founder, The Michelle McQuaid Group
Researcher · positive psychology, workplace wellbeing, leadership

Psychosocial Hazards & Culture of Care Components

Two components of the PERMA+ framework — Psychosocial Hazards and Culture of Care — were developed by Dr. Michelle McQuaid, founder of The Michelle McQuaid Group and a leading researcher and practitioner in applied positive psychology, workplace wellbeing, and leadership development.

Psychosocial Hazards

Dr. McQuaid designed this component to bring contemporary research on workplace psychological safety into the PERMA+ measurement framework. Drawing on regulatory developments, occupational health and safety research, and applied work with organisations, this component captures the systemic conditions that influence employee wellbeing — extending PERMA+ beyond individual flourishing into the organisational environment in which flourishing takes place.

Culture of Care

Dr. McQuaid developed this component to operationalise the relational, ethical, and leadership dimensions of organisational wellbeing. Informed by her research and practice with leaders globally, the Culture of Care component measures the practices, behaviours, and norms that support people to thrive sustainably at work.

Together, these two components extend PERMA+ from a primarily individual model of flourishing into a framework that also addresses the systemic and cultural conditions that enable wellbeing at scale. We gratefully acknowledge Dr. McQuaid’s contribution in translating these bodies of research into measurable, practical components of the framework.

A Foundation We Continue To Build On

Together

Dr. McQuaid and Dr. Kern translated the PERMAH theoretical framework — itself built on Dr. Seligman’s PERMA model — into a validated, practical instrument that has since been completed by participants worldwide and used to inform wellbeing strategy in workplaces, schools, and communities.

We acknowledge their work as the foundation on which the ongoing development of the PERMAH Wellbeing Survey continues to build.
