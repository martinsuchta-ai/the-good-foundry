How we measure

Wellbeing is measured at three levels.

At the individual (Me), the group (We), and the whole-of (Us) level. States of wellbeing at each level influence the overall sense of flourishing and doing well — it cannot be measured at the individual level alone.

Me Level

Intrapersonal

— our individual choices.

How each person cares for their own wellbeing — the daily choices, mindset, and self-care practices that shape an individual’s sense of flourishing.

We Level

Interpersonal

— relationships & teams.

Relationships and interactions between leaders and teams — how psychological safety, trust, recognition and connection are experienced day-to-day inside a group.

Us Level

Systemic

— across the organisation.

Interactions across the whole organisation — both internal (culture, systems, governance) and external (community, sector, supply chain) — that shape collective flourishing.

System mode

Full ME · WE · US

Measures all three levels. Recommended for organisation-wide programmes where culture, leadership and systems all need a signal.

Focused mode

ME · WE only

Measures the individual and team levels. A lighter survey for coaching engagements, single-team pilots, or programmes where the systemic lens is being measured separately.

In Partnership · LiveWell

Built In Partnership with LiveWell — the six pillars of ageing well.

Our newest bespoke edition. Tailored from our base Wellbeing Matters In Aging Well edition (WAW) and customised into a standalone LiveWell variant in partnership with the LiveWell aged-care programme. LiveWell’s six-pillar framework replaces PERMAH+ wholesale — Building Emotional Resilience · Knowing Me · Cultivating Inclusion & Belonging · Maintaining Purpose · Learning & Growing · Optimizing Health — with an additional Modifiable Risk Reflection section that surfaces the 14 evidenced cognitive-decline risk factors from the 2024 Lancet Commission so older adults and their care circles can see what is worth tending now.

Six LiveWell pillars

A bespoke framework, not a relabel.

Building Emotional Resilience · Knowing Me · Cultivating Inclusion & Belonging · Maintaining Purpose · Learning & Growing · Optimizing Health. Co-developed with the LiveWell programme to read in aged-care language end-to-end — care circle, GP, peer group, volunteering, legacy — rather than workplace vocabulary. Every dashboard card explains how the pillar interlocks with the other five so admins can see the SYSTEM, not just individual gauges.

Modifiable Risk Reflection

14 Lancet 2024 dementia & cognitive-ageing risks.

The bespoke addition that sets LiveWell apart from our base In Aging Well edition: a dedicated Modifiable Risk Reflection section walking respondents through the 14 evidenced modifiable risk factors from the 2024 Lancet Commission — hearing loss, depression, social isolation, physical inactivity, high blood pressure, smoking, alcohol misuse, obesity, diabetes, traumatic brain injury, air pollution, sleep problems, nutrition, and limited early-life education. For each, respondents reflect Have now / Unsure / Confident not. NOT a diagnostic — a reflective awareness tool that opens GP conversations and maps each risk back to the LiveWell pillar most likely to help.

Cohort risk profile + dashboards

Risk distribution, histogram, co-occurrence.

The LiveWell bank dashboard surfaces three cohort-level cards built on the Modifiable Risk data: per-factor risk distribution (which of the 14 risks the cohort flags most often), risks-flagged histogram (how many risks the average respondent carries), and top co-occurring risks (which pairs cluster together by Jaccard overlap). Programme leads can design aged-care initiatives around the cohort’s ACTUAL risk profile rather than assumed needs. Privacy-clipped at fewer than 5 respondents per cell.

Available now for the LiveWell programme. Tailored from our base Wellbeing Matters In Aging Well edition into a standalone bespoke LiveWell variant. The same In Partnership approach is available to any aged-care, community-ageing or retirement-living organisation whose context calls for its own framework, terminology and risk lens — LiveWell ships alongside the HEART Of Change Tool, our Stat Medical Education LLC ProQOL extension for first-responder research, and our Downer Psychosocial Safety variant as our current bespoke partnership stack. Bespoke Extension · Stat Medical Education LLC

Built with Stat Medical Education LLC — measuring first-responder wellbeing.

An adjunct extension to our base Wellbeing Matters @ Work edition (not a framework replacement). Built with Stat Medical Education LLC and Kyle Krebs at Grand Canyon University to power his doctoral thesis on Wellbeing and Compassion Satisfaction as Predictors of Medical First Responders’ Secondary Traumatic Stress and Burnout in the Northwest. The extension bolts a research-validated ProQOL instrument (Professional Quality of Life Scale by Beth Hudnall Stamm) onto our standard PERMAH+ workplace measurement — so first-responder organisations get both the broad wellbeing signal AND the focused compassion-fatigue lens in a single sitting, and researchers can import collected data straight into the platform’s analytics stack.

ProQOL adjunct

30 items. Three clinical scales.

The full Professional Quality of Life Scale (ProQOL) by Beth Hudnall Stamm, bolted onto base Wellbeing Matters @ Work as a dedicated custom section: Compassion Satisfaction (the pleasure derived from doing helping work well), Burnout (feelings of hopelessness and difficulty doing the job effectively), and Secondary Traumatic Stress (work-related exposure to traumatically stressful events). Custom 1–5 likert scale wired through the survey renderer; scoring helper publishes the three gauges with Stamm’s published cut-points (low / average / high bands).

First-responder screening

Pacific Northwest medical first responders.

Four screening custom questions on the Getting Started page narrow the cohort to the study’s population: Northwest United States residency, type of medical first responder (Firefighter / EMR / EMT / AEMT / Paramedic / Critical Care Paramedic / Flight Paramedic / Flight Nurse), organisation, and working capacity (Volunteer / Paid / Retired). Krebs / GCU Informed Consent (IRB-aligned) is presented before any item is shown, with consent acceptance time-stamped on every response.

Dashboard + data import

Three gauges, item-by-item distribution.

A bespoke ProQOL panel on the bank dashboard surfaces three gauges with Stamm’s cut-point bands plus a 30-item distribution grid so researchers can see the cohort’s answer pattern per question. The same surface accepts imported historical ProQOL collections — existing study data sets can be brought into the platform for unified analysis alongside fresh field collection. Export back out as the canonical wellbeing-platform JSON when the study completes.

Available now for Stat Medical Education LLC and Grand Canyon University. The same Bespoke Extension approach is available to any research team, professional body or workforce study that needs to bolt a validated instrument onto our base Wellbeing Matters platform — the ProQOL extension ships alongside our other bespoke implementations: LiveWell (aged-care six-pillar framework with the Lancet 2024 Modifiable Risk Reflection), the HEART Of Change Tool (built with The Change Lab on Dr. Michelle McQuaid’s framework), and a Psychosocial Safety variant for a major Australian infrastructure services group. New Bespoke · The HEART Of Change Tool

Built with The Change Lab — the HEART of navigating change.

Our newest bespoke edition. Built with The Change Lab (Dr. Michelle McQuaid’s team) on the HEART Framework — five evidence-based factors that help us regulate our nervous systems and create psychological safety during times of uncertainty. The 7-page survey measures each factor across three tiers (Individual, Team, Workplace) and surfaces a four-quadrant State of Change result so leaders can see exactly where their people are sitting on the Safety × Predictability matrix.

Five HEART factors

H · E · A · R · T.

Honour Feelings · Engage Purposefully · Appreciate Strengths · Reach Out · Take Tiny Steps. Each factor is measured at three tiers (Individual, Team, Workplace) so leaders can see whether the change-readiness gap sits with the person, the team, or the system around them.

State of Change quadrant

Capable. Challenged. Unsettled. Vulnerable.

Every respondent lands in one of four quadrants on a Safety (vertical) × Predictability (horizontal) matrix. The cohort dashboard shows the percentage in each quadrant against a configurable general-population baseline — so leadership can see at a glance whether their organisation is sitting high in Capable & Confident or dropping into Vulnerable & Uncertain.

Change AMPlifiers + Outcomes

Ability. Motivation. Psych Safety. Self-Compassion.

The dashboard also reports the four Change AMPlifiers (Ability / Motivation / Psychological Safety / Self-Compassion) and four Workplace Outcomes (Job Satisfaction / Performance / Safety / Wellbeing). Each respondent sees their own profile in the vault; admins see the cohort with PII-redacted free-text reflections.

Available now for The Change Lab clients. The same approach is available to any client whose context calls for a custom edition — the HEART Of Change Tool ships alongside our other bespoke implementations: LiveWell (In Partnership with the LiveWell aged-care programme, featuring the Lancet 2024 Modifiable Risk Reflection), our Stat Medical Education LLC ProQOL extension (built with Kyle Krebs at Grand Canyon University for first-responder research in the Pacific Northwest), and a Psychosocial Safety variant for a major Australian infrastructure services group.
