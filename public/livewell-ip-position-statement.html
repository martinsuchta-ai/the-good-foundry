1 Purpose and spirit

We are raising this early, openly, and in a spirit of partnership.

The work we are undertaking with Live Well on wellbeing measurement — and now extending into brain health — has moved beyond configuration and delivery. It is producing genuinely new intellectual property: original question sets, scoring logic, derivation methods, and interpretive frameworks that did not exist before this engagement.

We think it is healthier for both parties to say so now, plainly and early, than to discover a difference of understanding later. Nothing in this statement is intended to constrain Live Well's use of what we build together, or to alter the commercial relationship. Its purpose is simply to establish a clear, shared, dated understanding of who owns what — so that we can keep building with confidence.

We welcome Live Well's review, questions and response. We expect this to be a conversation.

2 Record date and effect

This statement records the position as at the effective date above.

Copyright and related rights in original work arise automatically on creation. This statement does not create those rights; it gives notice of them, identifies the body of work to which they attach, and fixes a shared reference point in time. Development work predating this date is included where it forms part of the body of work described below.

3 Scope of the work covered

This statement applies to TGF's wellbeing and brain health work, including:

  • The Wellbeing Matters @ suite and its PERMA Plus-based instruments, including derivatives of PERMA Plus such as the Live Well domains (which we acknowledge belong to Live Well — see section 5)
  • Brain Health Vitality
  • The Brain Health Profiler
  • Any further instruments, modules, extensions or derivative works that build on the above from time to time, whether developed for Live Well or otherwise

It applies to that work regardless of the format in which it is expressed — evaluation instruments, scoring engines, Insights outputs, documentation, code, dashboards, briefs and supporting materials.

4 What The Good Foundry owns

TGF asserts ownership of the intellectual property it has originated, including:

  • Question sets and item wording — the specific items, phrasing, response scales and sequencing authored by TGF
  • Scoring methodology — TGF's scoring algorithms, weightings, thresholds, banding, normative treatment and composite construction
  • Derivation of findings — the logic by which scores become interpretation: rules, decision paths, narrative generation, and the reasoning that turns data into an Insight
  • Frameworks, structures and labels — TGF-authored domain and pillar labels, taxonomies, and the models by which constructs are organised and related. This extends only to labels and structures originated by TGF; it does not extend to the Live Well domains or other Live Well-owned frameworks
  • Selection, arrangement and compilation — TGF's original curation of source material into a coherent, working instrument, including the choices about what to include, exclude, adapt and combine
  • Platform, code and technical implementation — the software, data structures, pipelines and delivery infrastructure
  • Know-how and methodology — the accumulated practice, judgement and process by which TGF designs, validates and evolves these instruments

This ownership extends to work developed in the course of, and informed by, the Live Well engagement.

5 What The Good Foundry does not claim

We think it is important to be equally clear about the limits of our claim. TGF does not assert, and does not seek to assert, ownership of:

  • Established academic models and scholarship in the public domain or published under open licence — including the PERMA model and its scholarly lineage, published dementia-risk and lifestyle indices, and the peer-reviewed literature on multidomain brain health intervention
  • Third-party licensed instruments — where an established clinical or psychometric instrument is used, it remains the property of its owner and is used under licence on its own terms
  • General wellbeing or brain health concepts — the underlying constructs, the science, and the general propositions of the field
  • The Live Well domains and other Live Well frameworks — the Live Well domains, being a derivative of the PERMA Plus model, are acknowledged by TGF as belonging to Live Well. TGF makes no claim to them, to their naming, or to any further derivative of PERMA Plus originated by Live Well. Where TGF's instruments measure, score or report against those domains, that work does not create any TGF interest in the domains themselves
  • Live Well's own material — Live Well's data, brand, content, client information and pre-existing intellectual property remain Live Well's throughout

Our claim is to what we have originated: the instrument, not the ideas it draws on, and not the frameworks our partners bring to it.

6 Live Well's position

Nothing in this statement is intended to disturb Live Well's ability to use what has been built. Subject to the terms to be settled in the formal agreement, our intention is that Live Well holds:

  • Full ownership of its own data and of the outputs generated for it
  • A licence to use the instruments and their outputs for its own purposes, on terms to be agreed
  • Continuity and certainty of access for the duration of the relationship

We are not seeking to restrict Live Well. We are seeking to be clear about the boundary.

7 Research and non-commercial use

We recognise that we build on scholarship that others made openly available to us, and we take that seriously.

Accordingly, TGF is open — at its discretion, on a case-by-case basis, and with appropriate acknowledgement — to granting licences to its intellectual property for research and non-commercial use. We would welcome conversations of that kind, including any that Live Well wishes to initiate or support.

8 Next steps

This statement is a plain-language expression of position, not a legal instrument. We propose:

  1. Live Well reviews this statement and comes back with questions, concerns or amendments
  2. We discuss and reach a shared understanding in principle
  3. Our respective legal advisers formalise that understanding into an appropriate agreement
  4. That agreement, once executed, supersedes this statement

We would rather have this conversation now, while the work is going well, than at a point where it is harder.

This statement is provided for discussion purposes. It is not legal advice and does not constitute a binding agreement between the parties.
